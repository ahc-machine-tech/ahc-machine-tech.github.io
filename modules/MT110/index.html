MT110 CNC G-Code

This course is designed for students with machine shop experience who want to learn how to set up, operate, and program Computer Numerical Controlled (CNC) machines. The course will cover a range of topics, including:

  • Introduction to Computer Aided Design and Manufacturing (CAD/CAM)
  • Cartesian Coordinate Systems
  • Basic CNC commands
  • CNC lathe operations
  • Cutting tools and materials
Students will gain practical experience through a series of hands-on lab exercises, including a project where they will design and create a safety sign for the lab. Students will also learn how to calculate feeds and speeds, create CNC part programs, and work safely and productively in a CNC machining facility. By the end of the course, students will be able to identify and select appropriate CNC cutting tools, set CNC work offsets, tool offsets, and cutter compensation and create CNC part programs to operate a variety of machine tools.
